Exercises and Techniques

Taking proper care of your physical and mental well-being is essential to living a fulfilled life and feeling good. Once you decide to make a change to improve your overall health, it can be difficult to figure out where to start. At Habitz, we aim to make this easier by highlighting the following exercises and techniques that you can implement in your everyday life.

Physical Exercises

Cardiovascular Exercises

  • Running
  • Swimming
  • Cycling
  • Brisk Walking
  • Dancing
  • High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)

Strength Training Exercises

  • Squats (weighted or unweighted)
  • Lunges
  • Push-ups

Flexibility and Stretching Exercises

  • Hamstring Stretch
  • Calf Stretch
  • Pilates

Functional Training Exercises

  • Step-Ups
  • Box Jumps
  • Burpees
  • Mountain Climbers
  • Kettlebell Swings

Core Strengthening Exercises

  • Planks
  • Russian Twists (dumbbells or medicine ball)
  • Leg Raises (hanging leg raises or Swiss ball)
  • Bicycle Crunches

Footnote: Always consult with a healthcare professional, such as your primary care provider (PCP), before starting a new exercise program. It's essential to tailor your routine to your fitness level and goals to ensure you safely get the highest benefit possible from your routine.

Mental Health Exercises

Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy Exercises

  • Cognitive Restructuring (reframing negative thoughts)
  • Journaling
  • Gratitude Log

Emotional Regulation Exercises

  • Grounding Techniques (focusing on the present moment)
  • Self-Compassion Exercises (practicing kindness and understanding towards yourself)
  • Creative Expression (expressing emotions through art, music, or writing)

Self-Care Exercises

  • Boundary Setting (establishing healthy boundaries with others)
  • Prioritization
  • Effective Time Management
  • Relaxation Techniques (e.g., deep breathing, meditation)

Social Connection Exercises

  • Active Listening (fully engaging with others in conversations)
  • Empathy Building (practicing understanding and compassion towards others)
  • Conflict Resolution (practicing healthy conflict resolution skills)
  • Joining a Social Group or Club

Self-Esteem and Confidence Exercises

  • Positive Self-Talk (practicing positive affirmations)
  • Goal Setting (setting and working towards achievable goals that make you feel better overall)
  • Self-reflection (Which of your traits are you most proud of?)

Footnote: These exercises are not a replacement for professional help or therapy. Although these techniques may improve your situation, if you're struggling with your mental health, please seek support from a qualified mental health professional.
